In linguistics, an adjective(abbreviated adj)is a word whose main role is to modify/describe
a noun or noun phrase. Its semantic role is to change information given by the noun.
Adjectives are one of the main parts of speech of the English language,
although historically they were classed together with nouns.
Certain words that were traditionally considered to be adjectives,
including the, this, my, etc., are today usually classed separately, as determiners.
